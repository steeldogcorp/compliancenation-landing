Driver SMS Consent

Last updated: March 11, 2026

How Drivers Opt In

Drivers opt in to receive SMS notifications by confirming their phone number and accepting SMS consent during the Compliance Nation driver onboarding process after receiving an invitation from their motor carrier.

SMS messages are used solely for compliance notifications related to DOT drug and alcohol testing programs and account alerts.

1 Carrier sends driver an invite
2 Driver confirms phone number
3 Driver checks SMS consent box
4 Driver submits onboarding form

SMS Consent During Onboarding

During onboarding, drivers are presented with the following consent prompt:

Receive Compliance Notifications by SMS

As shown during driver onboarding

Confirmation Message

Upon completing onboarding and consenting to SMS, drivers receive the following confirmation message:

Compliance Nation: You are subscribed to compliance notifications. Msg frequency varies. Msg & data rates may apply. Reply HELP for help or STOP to opt out.

What You're Agreeing To

By confirming your phone number and selecting the SMS consent option during onboarding, you consent to receive SMS text messages from Compliance Nation regarding:

  • DOT Drug & Alcohol Testing — Notifications about random testing selections, pre-employment test scheduling, and test result availability
  • Compliance Alerts — Reminders about expiring CDL, medical cards, annual reviews, and other required deadlines
  • Account Notifications — Onboarding links, document upload requests, and profile verification messages

Message Frequency

Message frequency varies depending on compliance events, testing selections, and account notifications, but typically does not exceed several messages per month unless required for time-sensitive compliance alerts such as random drug test notifications, which require prompt action under federal DOT regulations.

Message & Data Rates

Standard message and data rates may apply depending on your wireless carrier and plan. Compliance Nation does not charge for sending SMS messages, but your mobile provider may.

How to Opt Out

You can opt out of receiving SMS messages at any time by replying STOP to any message. After opting out, you will receive one final confirmation message. Please note that opting out of SMS notifications does not remove you from your carrier's compliance program — your employer's Designated Employer Representative (DER) will be responsible for contacting you directly regarding testing requirements.

Need Help?

Reply HELP to any message for assistance, or contact us at info@compliancenation.net.

SMS Commands

Drivers may manage SMS notifications using the following commands:

  • STOP — Unsubscribe from all SMS notifications
  • HELP — Receive assistance and support contact information

After replying STOP, drivers will receive a final confirmation message confirming their opt-out.

Privacy

Your phone number and personal information are handled in accordance with our Privacy Policy. We do not sell or share your phone number with third parties for marketing purposes. SMS consent is collected directly from the driver during onboarding and is not shared with third parties or used for marketing purposes. All driver information is protected under our compliance security standards.
